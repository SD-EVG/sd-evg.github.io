Bridging Facial Imagery and Vocal Reality:
Stable Diffusion-Enhanced Voice Generation

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

Voice generation in speech synthesis is challenging yet holds potential for versatile voices needed in entertainment and research. One primary obstacle in this area is the subjective nature and lack of diversity of voice descriptions. Our research seeks to represent emotions and speaker identities more objectively and diversely to address this issue. We introduce Stable Diffusion-Enhanced Voice Generation (SD-EVG), leveraging Stable Diffusion to generate realistic facial images for expressive voice synthesis. SD-EVG uses a transformer encoder, trained on multimodal features extracted by foundation models, and a fine-tuned Stable Diffusion decoder to imagine a speaker's face from their voice. SD-EVG also incorporates a KNN-based method that maps the identity and style of facial features to speech for voice generation. Experiments show voices from imaginary facial data more favorably capture style than text-based methods for the same descriptions.
